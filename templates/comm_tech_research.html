Academic Research & Future Technologies

Google/Nature Communications SpeakFaster (December 2024)

LLM-powered text entry achieving 29-60% typing speed improvement over baseline predictive keyboards. Works with existing Tobii hardware as software enhancement. Collaboration between Google Research and Team Gleason Foundation.

IEEE Wearable Gaze-Based Communication Study (2024)

See-through head-mounted display with integrated gaze estimation enabling communication without temporal, spatial, or physical restrictions‚Äîrepresenting the type of system that could eventually replace screen-based trackers.

Northwestern University/Shirley Ryan AbilityLab

Ongoing research on eye gaze characterization for assistive device control, including addressing the "Midas touch problem" (differentiating gaze for perception vs. interface operation). Study included ALS participants.

Emerging Technologies (12-36 Month Horizon)

  • GazeTrak acoustic sensing: Power-efficient alternative using sound instead of cameras
  • BlinkWise radio-wave tracking: University of Pennsylvania project for ultra-low-power eye tracking
  • ElectraSight hybrid EOG: Non-invasive smart glasses with onboard processing
  • EMG-based blink control glasses: Published in Biomedical Signal Processing 2023, tested with ALS patients
